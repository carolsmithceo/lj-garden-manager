GETTING STARTED

What is this app?Love Joy Farms Manager is your complete irrigation scheduling tool. It tracks every watering zone on your property, prevents schedule conflicts, calculates water usage, and syncs live across all your devices — PC, iPad, and iPhone — through the cloud.
First time opening?You'll be asked for your name. This is just so other users can see who made recent changes — it's stored only on your device. You can skip it, but entering your name is recommended.
Status pill (top of screen)● Live · Carol means you're connected and changes sync instantly. ↑ Saving… means a change is being written to the cloud. ○ Offline means no internet — changes are saved locally and will sync automatically when you reconnect.
Adding your first zoneClick + Add Zone in the top right. Fill in the Zone Name, how long it runs (Duration), how often (Frequency), which days, and what time it starts. End time fills in automatically. Click Save.
Editing a zoneDouble-click any row in the Schedule table, or click the Edit button at the end of the row. A panel opens with all the fields. Make your changes and click Save — all devices update within a second.
Deleting a zoneOpen the Edit panel for the zone, then click the red Delete Zone button at the bottom left of the panel. You'll be asked to confirm.

SCHEDULE TAB — YOUR MAIN WORKING VIEW

What it showsEvery watering zone in one table — name, group, map, duration, frequency, days, start/end times, and notes. This is where you manage all your zones.
Season selectorThe Spring / Summer / Fall / Winter buttons at the top dim any zones that are out of season. This is based on the Notes field — zones with "paused Dec–Feb", "Active May–Sep only", or similar text are automatically greyed out in the relevant season. Your data is not deleted, just visually quieted.
Summary cardsThe row of numbers below the season buttons shows: total zones, total run time (hours and minutes), average duration, number of greenhouse zones, greenhouse total duration, and any active errors.
Zone NameMust be unique — no two zones can share the same name. If a duplicate exists, both rows turn red as a warning.
GroupWhich overlap group(s) this zone belongs to. See the Overlap Groups section below for full explanation.
MapWhich map this zone appears on (Greenhouse, Outdoor, or any custom map you've created).
DurationHow many minutes this zone runs per session. Choose in 5-minute steps from 5 to 120 minutes.
FrequencyHow often the zone runs — Daily, Every 2 Days, 3×/Week, Weekly, etc.
DaysWhich days of the week this zone is scheduled. For example Mon/Wed/Fri or Tue/Thu/Sat.
Start TimeWhen this zone turns on, in HH:MM format (24-hour clock). Example: 06:30 for 6:30 AM, 14:00 for 2:00 PM.
End TimeAuto-calculated as Start Time + Duration. You can override it manually if needed. Orange = end is before start (a mistake). Red = overlaps with another zone.
NotesFree text — use it for crop info, seasonal reminders, maintenance notes, or anything useful. The season dimming logic reads this field, so phrases like "paused Dec–Feb" or "Active May–Sep only" will automatically trigger seasonal dimming.
Red highlightsRed zone name = duplicate name. Red start/end times = this zone's time overlaps with another zone it's not allowed to share time with. Fix by changing the start time, or assigning both zones to the same Overlap Group.
Orange end timeThe end time is at or before the start time in the same row. Usually means the end time was entered manually and is incorrect.

MAPS TAB — VISUAL LAYOUT OF YOUR ZONES

What it showsA visual floor plan of your irrigation zones. Each zone appears as a labelled box you can move around. Zones are colour-coded by which wall or side they sit on.
Multiple mapsEach property area gets its own map tab — Greenhouse, Outdoor, and any custom maps you create (e.g. Back Yard, Orchard). Switch between them using the MAP buttons at the top of the tab.
Adding a zone to a mapOpen the Edit panel for any zone (double-click it in Schedule). Set the Map field to the map you want it on. Set Map Side (Left, Right, Top, Bottom, or Centre) to choose which wall it sits on. The zone will appear on that map immediately.
Drag to repositionClick and hold any zone box, then drag it to a new position. Faint dashed rail guides show where zones will snap to. Release to drop — the position saves automatically to all devices. On iPad/iPhone, use your finger to drag.
Snap railsThe faint dashed lines on the left, right, and centre of the map are snap rails. Zones dragged near them will snap into place, keeping everything neatly aligned.
Auto-placed zonesZones that haven't been manually positioned show a dashed border and a tiny "auto" label. They are placed automatically in the next available slot. Drag them once to lock in their permanent position.
Colours by wallLeft wall = sage green · Right wall = lighter green · Top/North = teal · Bottom/South = indigo · Centre = orange. This makes it easy to read which side of the space a zone is in at a glance.
Group filterThe FILTER buttons (All, Group A, Group B, etc.) show only zones from that overlap group. New groups appear automatically when you create them.
Red error badgeA red circle with "!" on a zone means it has a validation error (duplicate name or time overlap). Click the zone to see the detail and fix it.
Creating a new mapWhen editing any zone, click the Map dropdown and choose + New map…. Type a name (e.g. "Back Yard"). That map tab appears immediately and zones assigned to it will show there.
Click a zone on the mapTapping/clicking a zone opens its detail card on the right — showing its full schedule, duration, gallons per week, and an Edit button to open the full editor.

WATER USAGE TAB — HOW MUCH WATER YOU'RE USING

What it showsFor every zone: flow rate (GPH), sessions per week, gallons per session, gallons per week, and gallons per month. Summary cards at the top show total weekly and monthly usage, daily average, and separate totals for greenhouse vs outdoor zones.
Flow Rate (GPH)Gallons Per Hour for each zone individually. Set this when editing a zone — look for the "Flow Rate (GPH)" field. Default is 1.0 GPH. A drip emitter might be 0.5 GPH; a sprinkler head might be 2–3 GPH. Ask your irrigation supplier if unsure.
Gallons per sessionCalculated as: (GPH ÷ 60) × Duration in minutes. So a zone running 20 minutes at 1 GPH uses 0.33 gallons per minute × 20 = ~6.6 gallons per session.
Monthly estimateWeekly gallons × 4.33 (average weeks per month). This is an estimate — actual usage varies with season and rainfall.

OVERLAP GROUPS — MANAGING SCHEDULE CONFLICTS

What is an overlap conflict?By default, no two zones can run at the same time. If Zone A runs 06:00–06:20 and Zone B runs 06:10–06:30, that's a conflict — the app flags both red. This prevents overloading your water pressure.
When is overlap OK?Sometimes you want zones to run simultaneously — for example all greenhouse deep beds are on the same valve and water together. Overlap Groups let you tell the app "these zones are allowed to overlap."
Leave blankNo overlap allowed. This zone must have a completely clear time slot — no other zone (outside its group) can overlap with it.
Enter a letter: AThis zone can overlap with any other zone that also has "A" in its group. Zones in Group A run together freely without triggering an error.
Enter multiple: A,CThis zone can overlap with any zone that has A or C in its group. Useful when a zone belongs to two different overlap scenarios.
Enter ALLThis zone can overlap with literally any other zone, regardless of group. Use sparingly — typically for a low-pressure zone like a misting system that doesn't affect water pressure.
Greenhouse beds exampleGH Deep Beds 1–9 are all Group A. They can all run at the same time without any conflict errors. GH Shallow Beds A–B are Group B — they overlap with each other but not with Group A zones.

CLOUD SYNC — SHARING ACROSS DEVICES

How it worksThe app connects to Google Firebase (a free cloud database) and saves every change in real time. Open the app on your iPad, PC, or iPhone — they all show the same data within about one second of any change being made.
Who can see my data?Only people who have this HTML file can access your data. The database is tied to your Google account ([email protected]). For now it's open to anyone with the file — consider adding a password later for extra security.
Crash recoveryEvery time you make a change, a crash file is written locally before the cloud save. If the app closes mid-save (crash, power loss, browser closed), the crash file survives. On next launch the app detects it, compares timestamps with the cloud, and pushes the newer version automatically. A yellow banner confirms recovery.
What happens offline?If you lose internet, the app keeps working using data saved locally on your device. A grey "○ Offline" pill appears at the top. Any changes you make are queued and will sync automatically the moment you reconnect — you won't lose anything. If offline at startup, a "Continue offline" link appears on the loading screen.
Manual Connect buttonIf the app can't connect automatically, a "⟳ Connect Now" button appears on the loading screen. Click it to retry. If connection still fails, click "Continue offline" to use the app with locally saved data only.
Reconnect button in headerWhen the app is offline, a "⟳ Reconnect" button appears in the top bar. Click it any time to retry the cloud connection.
Multiple users at onceUp to 3 users can edit simultaneously. The last person to save wins — there's no locking mechanism, so if two people edit the same zone at the exact same moment, the most recent save is kept. In practice this is rarely an issue.
Export JSON backupClick Export JSON (top right) to download a backup file of all your zone data. Save it somewhere safe. If you ever need to restore, click Import JSON and select the file.
Running on a new deviceJust open the HTML file in Safari (iPad/iPhone) or Chrome/Edge (PC). The app downloads all your data from the cloud automatically — no setup needed.
Opening on iPadOpen the file in Safari, then tap the Share button ⬆ → "Add to Home Screen" → Add. The app installs to your home screen and works like a native app, full screen with no browser bars.

SECURITY

Who can access my data?Only people who have this HTML file AND know the shared access token embedded in it. The Firestore database validates the token on every read and write — requests without it are rejected at the database level.
Is the API key safe?The Firebase API key is visible in the HTML source — this is normal and expected for Firebase web apps. The key alone doesn't grant access. Security is enforced by Firestore security rules that require the shared token.
XSS protectionAll user-entered data (zone names, notes, groups) is HTML-escaped before being displayed. This prevents malicious code from running even if someone were to write a crafted value to the database.
Content Security PolicyA CSP header restricts which scripts and resources the app can load, blocking unauthorized injections from unknown sources.
Keep the file privateDon't share the HTML file publicly — it contains the shared access token. Share it only with your 1–3 authorized users via AirDrop, email, or iCloud Drive directly.
Update the security rulesGo to Firebase Console → Firestore → Rules. Replace the current rules with the token-checking rules. See the "Cloud Sync" section above for instructions.

GREENHOUSE ZONES

Deep Beds 1–9 (Group A)4 ft × 8 ft × 2 ft deep raised beds. Each runs 20 minutes every 2 days at 1 GPH ≈ 20 gallons per session. Deep soil holds moisture well — daily watering would over-saturate the roots.
Shallow Beds A–B (Group B)4 ft × 4 ft × 6 inch deep beds. Each runs 10 minutes daily at 1 GPH ≈ 10 gallons per session. Shallow soil dries out quickly — daily watering is essential.
Year-roundGreenhouse zones are not paused in winter — the greenhouse maintains a frost-free environment. All 11 greenhouse zones run year-round.
Outdoor zones in winterMost outdoor zones are paused December through February due to freezing temperatures. The Season selector set to Winter will visually dim those zones. The schedule data is not deleted — just inactive until spring.

TIPS & BEST PRACTICES

Zero errors is the goalThe Errors count in the summary cards should always be 0. Red highlights mean something needs attention — fix them before the next watering cycle runs.
Notes field is powerfulWrite seasonal keywords in the Notes field and the app will dim zones automatically. Phrases that trigger dimming: "paused Dec–Feb", "Active Apr–Oct only", "Active May–Sep only", "Apr–Jun".
Back up regularlyCloud sync is reliable but it's good practice to Export JSON once a week and save it to your computer or Google Drive. Takes 10 seconds and protects against any account issues.
GPH accuracyWater usage estimates are only as accurate as your GPH values. Measure your actual flow rates by running each zone for 1 minute and collecting the water in a bucket. Update the GPH in each zone's Edit panel for precise numbers.
Map organisationCreate one map per distinct area — Greenhouse, Front Yard, Back Yard, Orchard, etc. Drag zones into position once and you'll have a permanent visual reference for your whole irrigation system.
Overlap groups for irrigation linesZones sharing the same physical irrigation valve or water line should be in the same overlap group — they physically run together and need the same start time. Zones on separate lines should have no group or different groups.
Something looks wrong?Try refreshing the page (F5 on PC, pull down in Safari on iPad). If data looks out of date, check the status pill — if it shows "○ Offline", your device isn't connected to the internet. Data will sync when you reconnect.
